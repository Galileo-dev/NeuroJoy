Parts of a Neuron

Cell body

AKA the soma. This portion of the neuron receives information from dendrites. It also contains the cell’s nucleus.

Nucleus

'The control center of the cell'. It stores DNA, and regulates the production of protein within the cell.

Cell Membrane

A selectively-permeable barrier that surrounds the cell. It allows certain substances to enter and leave the cell.

Dendites

Dendrite comes from the Greek word for tree, due to their shape. They are covered in receptors so they can recieve messages

Axon hillock

Connects the cell body to the axon. It acts like a floodgate. If the soma recieves a certain threshold of signals, it conducts an impulse down the axon

Axon

Tube-like structure that carries impulses away from the cell body, towards axon terminals

Schwann cells

Myelin is found in Schwann cell's fatty membranes. These cells wrap around the axon, creating several layers of insulation, known as a myelin sheath

Myelin Sheath

Provides electrical insulation so that the electric current in the axon is not lost to the surrounding tissue and it speeds up the impulse

Axon terminals

Bulbous swellings that appear where the axon branches out. They make connections on target cells. AKA nerve terminals.
