What is the

Nervous System?

First off, what even is the nervous system? Well, it's kinda important as it controls everything you do! Breathing, walking, thinking, feeling - you name it!!

Function of the Nervous System

One thing you need to know is that organisms (like us!) need to be aware of our surroundings in order to survive. It’s our nervous system’s job to detect and respond to changes inside and outside our body.

These changes are referred to as stimuli! These can include a flash of light, touching something hot, or your alarm going off in the morning

Structure of the Nervous System

The nervous system can be broadly broken into two parts - the central nervous system and the peripheral nervous system

Central Nervous System (CNS)

The central nervous system consists of the brain and the spinal cord

This is where analysis of information takes place

Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)

The peripheral nervous system contains nerves branching from the CNS to all parts of the body

It is responsible for delivering messages to and from the CNS
