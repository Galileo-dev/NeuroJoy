Electricity

To properly understand how neurons function we gotta dive into some physics and chemistry real quick

Anatomy of an Atom

Atoms are teeeeeny particles that make up- everything! Atoms are composed of even tinier particals called subatomic particles.

The center of an atom has a nucleus consisting of protons and neutrons. Protons have a positive charge, neutrons have no charge, resulting in the nucleus being overall, positively charged. Electrons orbit the nucleus, and they have a negative charge.

Electrostatic force

Electrons stay in orbit as opposite charges attract. On the flip side, same charges repel. The attraction or repulsion of charged ions is referred to as electrostatic force.

If an atom either gains or loses an electron it becomes charged. Charged atoms are called ions.

Cations

If an atom loses an electron, it becomes positively charged, since electrons are negative. This is called a cation.

Anions

If an atom gains an electron, it becomes negatively charged. This is called an anion.

Voltage

Voltage is the difference in electrical potential between two points. Now that we covered electrostatic forces, we can now understand electrical potential.

Let’s imagine there's a difference in charge between two points in space. In this example A is more negative than B. The potential created by A and B would cause a positively charged ion to move towards A, the more negative charge.

A negatively charged ion would move towards B, the more positive charge.

Voltage tells us the strength of this potential difference, and is measured in volts (V). Although neurons are reaaaally tiny so with them we use milivolts (mV).

Low Voltage

There's not that big of a difference between the positive and negative charges here. This means the voltage is low.

High Voltage

There is a crazy high difference between the positive and negative charges here, resulting in a very high voltage!!!
