Neurons

Neurons are the building blocks of the nervous system! They carry electrical messages around the body. These electrical messages are also called impulses, action potentials, and nerve impulses.

Neuron Classes

Brilliant! Now that we covered what a neuron is, let’s look at different types! Right now we're gonna focus on three classes—the sensory neuron, motor neuron, and interneuron

Sensory Neuron

First up we have the sensory neuron! They carry messages from the sense organ to the central nervous system (CNS)

Motor Neuron

Next we have the motor neuron. It carries impulses from the CNS to muscles and glands

Interneuron

Lastly, the interneuron. It is only found in the CNS and they are the most numerous class of neurons. It’s job is to connect one neuron to another within the CNS.

How Neurons Work Together

Okay so let’s walk through a scenario to better visualise how these neurons interact with each other.

Imagine you are taking cupcakes out of their tin and you burn your hand
The sensory neuron detects that the oven is a really high temperature and sends this info the the CNS
The signal would travel to interneurons in your spinal cord
Some interneurons would signal to your motor neurons, this results in you pulling your hand away from the hot tin
Other interneurons fire the signal up to neurons in your brain, where it can be perceived as pain
